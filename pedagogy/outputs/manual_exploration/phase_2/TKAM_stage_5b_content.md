**Why To Kill a Mockingbird's Narrative Technique Creates Dual Consciousness in Readers**

If you've wondered why TKAM feels different from other first-person memory narratives, the answer lies in Harper Lee's use of what literary scholars call dual consciousness. Unlike typical retrospective narration where an adult simply recalls childhood events, Scout simultaneously presents childhood innocence and adult moral understanding in the same moment, creating a unique reading experience where you feel both wonder and judgment at once. This effect emerges through specific scenes like the ancestry flashback and interrupted moral judgments, but it's actually part of a larger pattern called the Maturing Moral Witness technique—which I break down completely in my comprehensive analysis guide.
