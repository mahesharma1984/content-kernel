Orbital

by Samantha Harvey

Knowledge: What the Book Does

Cosmic Contemplation

The narrator has omniscient access to multiple consciousness flows. This means we can see inside different characters' minds — their thoughts, feelings, inner experiences. But the narrator also maintains an objective philosophical stance. They step back from the individual emotions to observe the bigger picture. These two techniques combine to create something specific: • A meditative observation of human experience • Set against cosmic scale Ask yourself: Why does Harvey give us both intimate access to thoughts AND distant philosophical perspective? The effect is contemplative — we feel close to the characters while simultaneously seeing how small human concerns appear against the vastness of space.

Reader Effect: Readers experience two perspectives at the same time: • **Intimacy**: we're close to individual human thoughts • **Detachment**: we see from a vast, cosmic perspective This creates contemplative distance. What is contemplative distance? It means we can think deeply about what we're observing rather than just feeling it. The technique works like this: • Close perspective → we connect emotionally • Distant perspective → we step back and analyse • Together → we both feel AND think Ask yourself: Why does this distance matter? The dual perspective elevates mundane experience to universal significance. Let's unpack this complex idea: • "Mundane experience" = ordinary, everyday moments • "Universal significance" = truths about all of humanity • "Elevates" = lifts something up, makes it more important The process works like this: One person's ordinary moment → becomes a symbol → represents something true for everyone. This is how literature transforms the specific into the universal — individual experience becomes a mirror for human nature itself.
